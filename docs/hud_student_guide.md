# Cadet's Guide: How to Use the Blue-Water Rover HUD!
## Welcome to the Mission Control Center!

Hello, future roboticist! You are looking at the **Heads-Up Display (HUD)** for the Blue-Water Rover—an autonomous solar catamaran sailing 700 miles along the Florida coast. 

Think of this dashboard as your spaceship control panel. Here is how to access it, read the dials, and take command of the vessel!

---

## 1. How to Launch the HUD

1.  **Start the Engine**: The server code should already be running. If not, open your computer terminal and type:
    ```bash
    python3 src/main.py
    ```
2.  **Open the Viewscreen**: Open any web browser (like Chrome or Safari) and go to this web address:
    `http://localhost:5000`

You will see a glowing, sci-fi style dashboard. Let’s learn what all the glowing parts mean!

---

## 2. Reading Your Instruments

The screen is split into three main sections:

```
+---------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
|                               HUD SCREEN LAYOUT                                 |
+---------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
|   SECTION 1: Power Vitals      |   SECTION 2: Radar & Map   | SECTION 3: Console|
|   - Battery Level (Green Ring) |   - Rotating Compass Rose  | - Big Red KILL    |
|   - Solar Input vs Motor Draw  |   - Obstacle Sonar (D455)  | - Waypoint Form   |
|   - Rudder & Throttle Bars     |   - SVG Florida Coast Map  | - Live Packet Log |
+---------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
```

### 1. The Energy Tank (Section 1 - Left)
*   **Battery Level (Green Ring)**: Shows how full the batteries are. 100% means fully charged!
*   **Net Balance (Amber Ring)**: Tells you if you are making energy or losing it. 
    *   *Positive (+)*: Making power (sun is bright!).
    *   *Negative (-)*: Draining power (motors are running fast!).
*   **Solar Harvest**: Watts generated by solar panels.
*   **Propulsion Draw**: Watts consumed by the thruster motor.
*   **Throttle & Rudder Bars**: Visual indicators showing how much power the motor is using and how far left or right the rudder is turned.

### 2. Scanning the Sea (Section 2 - Center)
*   **The Compass Rose**: A circle showing North, East, South, and West.
    *   *Blue Pointer*: The direction the boat is heading.
    *   *Amber Pointer*: The direction of the next waypoint target.
*   **Proximity Sonar**: A half-circle divided into **Left, Center, and Right** wedges. This represents the **Intel RealSense D455** camera's eyes:
    *   *Green/Clear*: No obstacles.
    *   *Yellow*: An obstacle is detected within 10 meters (caution!).
    *   *Red*: An obstacle is extremely close (< 6 meters). The autopilot will swerve automatically to avoid it!
*   **Coastline Map**: An interactive map tracking the boat as it sails from Charleston, SC, around the Florida Keys, to Tampa Bay!

### 3. Taking the Helm (Section 3 - Right)
*   **The Big Red Button (EMERGENCY MOTOR KILL)**: Clicking this immediately cuts all motor power. The boat will float safely in place.
*   **Switch to Auto / Manual**: Toggle between manual RC remote steering or letting the autopilot steer.
*   **Inject Waypoint**: Enter target Latitude and Longitude numbers (global coordinates) and click "Inject" to send the boat to a new destination!
*   **Serial Log**: The scrolling green text at the bottom is the raw messaging feed. This is the code sending telemetry diagnostic packages (beginning with `$TEL:DIAG`) back to the base station.

## 3. Mission Experiments to Try!

Become a test pilot by trying these operations:

1.  **Test Autonomous Sailing**:
    *   Click the **SWITCH TO AUTO** button.
    *   Watch the mode badge turn green and read `AUTO`.
    *   Look at the compass: the boat will automatically steer toward the target waypoint.
2.  **Avoid an Obstacle**:
    *   In the map or simulator view, watch the proximity sonar sectors change color as mock buoys and land approach.
    *   Watch the rudder bar deflection respond to steer away from the yellow/red zones!
3.  **Perform a Rescue Stop**:
    *   Press the **EMERGENCY MOTOR KILL** button.
    *   Watch the HUD header display `EMERGENCY KILLED` in red text. The throttle bar will immediately drop to 0.
    *   To start sailing again, click the grey **RESET SHUTDOWN** button.
